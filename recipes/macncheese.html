Viking Mac & Cheese

Description

Delicious cheesy baked mac and cheese. This recipe uses ten ingredients, including sharp cheddar and Jarlberg cheese. The Jarlberg cheese melts well and gives the dish a mild nutty flavor. For best results, you should buy blocks of cheese and shred them yourself. The coating used in processed shredded cheeses makes them clump when melting.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. elbow pasta
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/2 flour
  • 1 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 2 1/2 cups half and half
  • 4 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 cups shredded Jarlsberg cheese
  • 1/2 tbsp. salt
  • 1/2 tsp. black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp. paprika

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees F. Grease a 9x13" baking dish and set aside.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. When boiling, add pasta and cook 1 minute less than the package directions for al dente. Drain and drizzle with olive oil to keep from sticking.
  3. While water is coming to a boil, shred cheeses and mix together, then divide into three piles: 3 cups for the sauce, 1 1/2 cups for the middle layer, and 1 1/2 cups for the topping.
  4. Melt butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Sprinkle in flour and whisk to combine. Cook for 5 minutes, whisking often. Slowly pour in 2 cups of the milk and half and half while whisking until smooth. Slowly pour in the remaining milk and half and half and whisk until smooth.
  5. Continue to whisk over medium heat for 5 minutes or until thickened slightly.
  6. Remove from heat. Stir in spices and the 3 cups of cheese for the sauce until melted and smooth.
  7. Combine drained pasta with cheese sauce in a large mixing bowl. Stir to mix fully. Pour half o the pasta mixture onto the prepared baking dish. Top with 1 1/2 cups of shredded cheeses, then top that with the remaining pasta mixture.
  8. Top with remaining cheese and bake for 15 minutes or until cheese is bubbly and light golden brown.
